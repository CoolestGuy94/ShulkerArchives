Introduction

So, you've found the Archives, maybe even downloaded an NBT File or two, and are wondering how to use them.

Or maybe you know how to use them, but are curious about editing and creating your own files.

Hopefully this page will show you everything you need to know about everything NBT :)

Check the Sidebar on the left for a clickable overview of this page.

Prerequisites

To get started with anything NBT related, you'll need a few basic mods:

  • NBT Editor ≥1.10.0 by mega12345mega
  • Pick Block Pro by Sjouwer
  • Adaptive Tooltips ≥1.1.1 by isXander

    • These three mods are absolutely essential for working with NBT, and all users of the Archives should have them.

    More advanced and niche Mods (and external applications) will be listed below in their relevant sections, and all Resources can be found at the bottom of this page in the Index, as well as on the Resources page.

    Using .nbt Files

    The core of the Archives: .nbt files. But how do you use them?

    Well, it's simple.

    If you followed the prior section, you should have the NBT Editor mod installed in your Minecraft instance.

    To use a .nbt file, open your inventory, and then drag-and-drop the .nbt file on to your Minecraft screen.

    NBT Editor should automatically add the Kit or Item contained in the .nbt file into your Inventory, using the first available slot.

    Using SNBT

    The secondary core of the Archives: SNBT. But how do you use it?

    There are a variety of ways to use S(tringified)NBT (that's what SNBT stands for!), but each has its own issues. We'll go over three of the most common methods below: NBT Editor, IBE Editor, and Meteor Client.

    Please note that Items containing NaN/Inf attributes will NOT retain those attributes when obtained from any source that uses SNBT. Only Hotbars and .nbt files can successfully save Nan/Inf attributes.

    NBT Editor

    This method doesn't involve any additional mods besides those in Prerequisites, and is in the middle in terms of difficulty and ease-of-use.

    To use the SNBT, first copy the SNBT using the provided button.

    Once copied, open your inventory and hover over the item you wish to add the SNBT to, then click the spacebar.

    In the NBT Editor menu choose the button labeled 'Expand', remove any SNBT in the form it brings up, then paste the SNBT in.

    Choose 'Done', 'Save Changes', and exit the Menu.

    Voila, you have your new Kit or Item!

    IBE Editor

    For this method, you will of course need IBE Editor.

    If you need to use SNBT, IBE Editor is the best option. It has the bonus functionality of being excellent for NBT Editing and Item Creation, while also being fairly easy to use to add or edit SNBT.

    To use the SNBT, copy the SNBT either manually, or with the copy button provided on the page.

    Then, while holding the item you wish to edit, open IBE's 'SNBT Editor' menu and click right before the closing curly brace.

    Type ,tag: and then paste the SNBT in after it. Choose 'Done'.

    Voila, you have your new Kit or Item!

    Meteor Client

    For this method, you will of course need Meteor Client.

    Meteor Client is easily the worst option of the three for SNBT (or any NBT for that matter), but it does work, and many like to use it.

    First, copy the SNBT using the provided button.

    Once copied, hold the item you wish to apply the SNBT to, then open chat and type .nbt paste.

    Hit enter, and it should be applied.

    Voila, you have your new Kit or Item!

    A Note on Data Merge

    A common command associated with NBT is /data merge.

    While the SNBT provided by these Archives is, to a degree, useable with this command, it has a major drawback:

    Name and Lore of the containing storage (Chest, Barrel, Shulker, etc) will not be added.

    In addition to this, using the /data merge command for Shulker Kits is an ordeal in and of itself, involving Command Blocks, Fiddly Coordinates, and significant manual editing of the SNBT.

    For these reasons, instructions on this command will NOT be provided here, and support will not be given to those seeking to use this command.

    For those who will say "but my Vanilla Client only supports /data merge!", too bad. There is zero reason not to use a modded client when working with NBT, even if you're just browsing Shulker Kits and not actually editing items.

    Creating your own Kits & Items

    You've got other peoples Kits and Items, and now you want to make your own... but how can you do that?

    Well, while we can't tell you what to create, we can give you some pointers and tools to use.

    IBE Editor

    Mentioned above under 'Using SNBT', IBE Editor is one of the best mods currently available for editing Items; Whether you want to quickly adjust the Name and Lore, fiddle around with Enchantments and Attributes, or do a deep dive into the NBT Tags, IBE Editor is the mod to do it with.

    FZMM

    FZMM is a curious little mod, offering a handful of fairly niche but incredibly useful utilities.

    The most useful of its utilities for Item-making however, is the Text Format module, which offers an amazingly intricate and detailed menu to create text, while also being quite intuitive. The cherry on top? It can export your created text as Lore, Item Name, or just copy it to your clipboard!

    Your Imagination

    Many things are important in Item Making... but above all else, use your imagination. Look at other peoples kits as inspiration, not goals.

    Don't be discouraged if you don't understand all the aspects of NBT Editing, or are confused when someone mentions things like NaN Attribute Amounts or Damage Frames; It takes time to learn about the intricacies of NBT Editing.

    Remember, if you ever have questions, feel free to ask on The Shulker Archives Discord server; Even if someone here doesn't know the answer, we'd be more than happy to help find the answer.

    Storing Kits & Items

    You now have obtained Kits and Items from the Archives, and hopefully created your own... but now how can you save them?

    Placing them all down in one spot is a pain, and Hotbars are both limited and carry the very real danger of accidentally erasing one item while saving another.

    The mods you've installed along the way all come with some form of Storage or another, from NBT Editors 'Client Chest', to IBE Editors 'Vault', but all of them have their bugs and issues that prevent them from being the best.

    What, then, is the best? Why JEI of course.

    JEI aka Just Enough Items, has an excellent feature called "Bookmarks".

    To use this, simply hover over anything in your Inventory, and press 'a'.

    Voila! The item is now saved in the 'Bookmarks' tab on the left.

    To obtain a bookmark, simply enable 'Cheat Mode' by CTRL + Left-Clicking the wrench in the lower right-hand side, and then Left or Right-Click a bookmark to obtain a copy of it.

    Please note that Items containing NaN/Inf attributes will NOT retain those attributes when obtained from Bookmarks, or any similar source that uses SNBT. Only Hotbars and .nbt files can successfully save Nan/Inf attributes.

    Exporting Kits & Items

    There are two main ways to export your Kits and Items, each specific to what format you're exporting to.

    Exporting as .nbt files

    The best way to export it (and the core method of these Archives) is as a .nbt file.

    To do so, you will need NBT Editor installed.

    While holding the Kit or Item you wish to export, open chat and type /nbt export file.

    Voila! It is now exported as a .nbt file, located in .minecraft/nbteditor/exported/

    Exporting as SNBT

    The secondary method of export is as SNBT.

    To do so, you will need NBT Editor or IBE Editor or Meteor Client.

    Exporting SNBT via NBT Editor

    Open your inventory, hover over the Kit or Item you wish to export, and press spacebar.

    Choose 'Expand', then click onto the form it brings up, press CTRL+A and then CTRL+C to copy the SNBT to your clipboard.

    Voila! You now have the SNBT of that Kit or Item.

    Exporting SNBT via IBE Editor

    Hold the Kit or Item you wish to export, and open the SNBT Editor by pressing the assigned hotkey (default is R)

    Click onto the form it brings up, press CTRL+A and then CTRL+C to copy the SNBT to your clipboard.

    Please note that IBE prefaces SNBT with additional data, e.g: {Count:1,id:"minecraft:white_shulker_box",tag:} which must be removed or altered prior to using the copied SNBT.

    Voila! You now have the SNBT of that Kit or Item.

    Exporting SNBT via Meteor Client

    Hold the Kit or Item you wish to export, and open chat.

    Type .nbt copy in chat.

    Voila! You now have the SNBT of that kit or Item.
